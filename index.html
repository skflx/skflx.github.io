Surgeon-scientist in training in the PNW

Progressive sensorineural hearing loss since 13, and got a cochlear implant at the end of medical school. I'm now learning to operate on the head and neck — including the auditory system and otology — which means I can ask what something sounds like from both sides of the electrode.

That position drives my primary research. I'm investigating binaural pitch fusion as a pre-surgical predictor of bilateral implantation outcomes in bimodal cochlear implant users: who actually benefits from bilateral implantation, and can we predict it before surgery?

My second axis is emerging technologies, surgical education, and bringing otolaryngology into the 21st century. I grew up in Kathmandu, Michigan, and Oregon, trained in Nevada and New Haven, and ended up back in the Pacific Northwest. Outside the hospital I like putting ink on paper, having books in my hand, picking heavy things off the floor, and frolicking through the forests, hills, and waterfalls of the PNW.
