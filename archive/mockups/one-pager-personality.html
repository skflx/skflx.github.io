the short version

A bit about me

My background is in neuroscience, and my own experience with hearing loss shapes how I think about both clinical care and research in hearing technologies. Day to day I'm drawn to surgical outcomes in otolaryngology and to the data tools and cognitive frameworks that make surgical learning better.

Outside the hospital I keep my hands busy — ink on paper, strings under my fingers, trails under my boots, and a barbell on the floor. I think the balance between technical precision and creative expression is the whole point.
