About Me

My journey in medicine, research, and beyond

My Story

I'm a resident otolaryngologist at Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU), where I blend medical practice with a passion for research and innovation. My journey began in Nepal and has taken me through Michigan, Oregon, Nevada, and Connecticut before bringing me back to the Pacific Northwest.

With a background in neuroscience and a deep interest in hearing technologies, I bring a unique dual perspective as both a physician and someone with personal experience with hearing loss. This drives my commitment to improving patient outcomes through innovative surgical techniques and technology development.

My research focuses on otolaryngology outcomes, particularly in areas related to cochlear implants and craniofacial trauma. I've published multiple peer-reviewed papers and presented at national conferences, always seeking ways to advance our understanding and treatment approaches.

Beyond medicine, I'm an artist, musician, and outdoor enthusiast who values the balance between technical precision and creative expression.

Research Interests

Cochlear Implants

Studying outcomes and cortical adaptations in cochlear implant patients using functional near-infrared spectroscopy.

Speech Processing

Developing improved algorithms for speech-in-noise processing in hearing assistive devices.

Craniofacial Trauma

Identifying factors associated with improved outcomes in craniofacial trauma surgery.

Medical Education

Exploring innovative methods for surgical education, including live-streaming procedures.

Neuroscience

Clinical Outcomes
