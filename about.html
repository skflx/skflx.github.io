About Me

My journey in medicine, research, and beyond

My Story

I'm a resident otolaryngologist in the Pacific Northwest, where I combine medical practice with a passion for research and pedagological and technological innovation. My journey began in Nepal and has taken me through Michigan, Oregon, Nevada, and Connecticut before bringing me back to the Pacific Northwest.

With a background in neuroscience and a deep interest in hearing technologies, I bring a unique perspective as both a physician and someone with personal experience with hearing loss.

My research focuses on surgical outcomes in otolaryngology, with a more recent focus on emergent data technologies in medical education and surgical training.

Beyond medicine, I'm an artist, musician, and outdoor enthusiast who values the balance between technical precision and creative expression.

Research Interests

Cochlear Implants

Studying outcomes and cortical adaptations in cochlear implant patients using functional near-infrared spectroscopy.

Speech Processing

Working on improving speech recognition in challenging listening environments by enhancing software and hardware solutions in hearing devices.

Craniofacial Trauma

Identifying factors associated with improved outcomes in craniofacial trauma surgery.

Medical Education

Exploring innovative methods for surgical education, including live-streaming procedures.

Neuroscience

Investigating the neural basis of sensory processing and adaptation.

Clinical Outcomes

Analyzing factors that influence surgical outcomes and complication rates in otolaryngology procedures.
