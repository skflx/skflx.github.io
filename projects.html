Research Projects

My research focuses on improving surgical outcomes and understanding neurosensory processing in hearing disorders. Below are key research areas I've contributed to:

  • Cortical Activity Changes in Cochlear Implant Patients
    Principal Investigators: Nofrat Schwartz, MD and Vincent L Gracco, PhD

    Utilizing functional near-infrared spectroscopy to assess changes in cortical activity of cochlear implant patients over time, combined with retrospective investigation on institutional outcomes.

  • HPV Awareness Intervention in Otolaryngology
    Co-first author with Williams LC, under PI: Benjamin L Judson, MD, MBA

    Designed and implemented a pilot physician intervention aimed to increase patient HPV awareness and surveyed patients on otolaryngology-centric HPV knowledge pre- and post-intervention.

  • Head and Neck Surgery Complications
    Principal Investigator: Benjamin L Judson, MD, MBA

    Characterized and identified risk factors for complications in various head and neck surgeries, with specific focus on factors associated with prolonged inpatient stay after total laryngectomy.

  • Craniofacial Trauma Outcomes
    Principal Investigator: Yan H Lee, MD

    Investigated the effects of alcohol-use in craniofacial traumas and identified factors associated with worse outcomes in craniofacial surgeries.

  • Medical Education via Surgical Livestreaming
    Principal Investigator: Yan H Lee, MD

    Evaluated the utility of live-streaming surgical electives for medical education, demonstrating enhanced exposure to surgical specialties for preclinical students.

Emergent Technology Projects

I'm passionate about developing new technologies to address challenges in hearing healthcare and surgical practice.

Speech-in-Noise Processing Technology

2022 - Present Hearing Technology

Working on improving speech recognition in challenging listening environments by enhancing both software and hardware solutions in hearing devices. This technology aims to address one of the most significant challenges for individuals with hearing loss.

The project focuses on finding new approaches to better isolate speech from background noise, particularly in complex acoustic environments with multiple speakers or competing sounds.

Surgical Livestreaming Platform

Designed and implemented a technical system for livestreaming surgical procedures to medical students, including audio/video setup and sterile exoscope mounting for open procedures.

Clinical Projects
